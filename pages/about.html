About Me

I was born and raised in Northwest Ohio. After graduating from high school in the area, I went to study at the University of Toledo.

College was confusing, difficult, and also very fun (side note for those that notice, I'm a fan of Oxford commas; I do not apologize). I changed majors several times. I knew what I wanted to study, but I knew that if I pursued it, I'd have to figure out my career path after graduation. Passion ended up beating practicality and I left UT with a Philosophy degree and just as much confusion as when I entered college.

My life after college began with the hospitality job that I worked throughout my time at UT. I spent less than a year there and then pivoted into a substitute teaching job. I also took on a remote intern position studying mindfulness at the Unviersity of Michigan at this time (though this position was unpaid and ended up not being what I was looking for).

The teaching role was one of the most fulfilling jobs I've ever had. It was also one of the worst jobs I've ever had for my mental health. It took one really bad day and I decided right then and there that I couldn't handle working in a school. During my lunch break that day, I researched what I could study for free to put me on a better career path for myself. That moment was the beginning of my coding journey. I spent a school year there while also learning to code and then left with no job in mind. Luckily, my mom was a mail carrier at the time and showed me a job posting for a part-time clerk position.

The Postal Service also had its ups and downs. Ultimately, the position was useful for what it was. It kept some money in my pocket and helped me pay off my student loans. With it being a part-time job, I was able to dedicate a lot of time during those days to study web development.

When I felt ready enough, I put together a portfolio, I created my first "all by myself" web project, and I slapped that project on the portfolio (as well as a little weather app I made, so I at least had two projects on the portfolio).

I applied to over one hundred companies over seven months. Of those applications, three companies reached back out to me. Two of them brought me in for two rounds of interviews. Unfortunately, both of those companies didn't reach out to me after those interviews. The third company brought me in for two interviews and offered me a job!

I left the Postal Service full of excitement for what lied ahead!

Now I work as a Project Developer learning about "all things coding" every single day. I love this profession and I still continue to study web development on my own when I have the time and mental capacity to do so.
